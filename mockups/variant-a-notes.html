Q2 Strategy

The wedge is the demand gate: 5 power users, 3 say they'd pay. Everything before that is just building the demo.

Local-first is the moat. Features are the demo — they're copyable. What isn't copyable is that your whole brain lives on your own disk and never leaves. Link this to #positioning and #moat.

Next milestone is TTS so the brain can talk back while you walk.

What pricing models would fit a local-first app where I can't see usage?
Since you can't meter usage server-side, lean on value-based tiers: a free local tier, then a one-time license or a flat subscription that unlocks sync and TTS. Charging per-token fights the local-first promise.

Could vault size be a fair axis?
It's legible and scales with the heavy users, but it punishes exactly the power users you want as evangelists. A capability tier (sync, mobile, voice) reads cleaner.
