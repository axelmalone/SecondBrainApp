# Notes on Stoicism

The central idea is the dichotomy of control: some things are up to us — our
judgments, our choices, our effort — and some things are not, like other
people's opinions or the weather. Peace comes from pouring energy only into the
former and accepting the latter.

Marcus Aurelius wrote his journal to himself, not for publication, reminding
himself each morning that he would meet difficult people and should not be
surprised or knocked off course by them.

The obstacle becomes the way: every setback is raw material for practicing
virtue.
