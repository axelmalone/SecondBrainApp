# Growing Tomatoes

Pinch out the suckers — the little shoots in the joint between the stem and a
branch — so the plant pours its energy into fruit instead of foliage.

Water deeply at the base in the morning and never on the leaves; wet foliage
overnight invites blight and other fungal disease. A thick mulch keeps moisture
even and stops soil from splashing spores up onto the plant.

Feed with a potassium-rich fertilizer once the first flowers set, and stake the
vines before they sprawl.
