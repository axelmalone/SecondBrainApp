# Sourdough Notes

Feed the starter twice a day until it reliably doubles within four hours; that
springiness is how you know the wild yeast is awake enough to leaven a loaf.

The bulk ferment is where the magic happens. Leave the dough at room temperature
for roughly four to six hours, folding it every half hour for the first two,
until it grows puffy and full of bubbles. Underproofed bread comes out dense.

Bake in a screaming-hot Dutch oven with the lid on for the first twenty minutes
so the trapped steam gives you a dramatic oven spring and a blistered crust.
