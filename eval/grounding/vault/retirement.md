# Retirement Planning

The single biggest lever is starting early so compounding has decades to work.
A low-cost broad-market index fund beats almost every actively managed fund over
a thirty-year horizon once you account for fees.

Max out the employer match first — it is free money — then funnel the rest into
a tax-advantaged account. Automating the contribution each payday removes the
temptation to spend it.

Rebalance once a year and otherwise ignore the noise; selling in a downturn is
how most people lock in their losses.
