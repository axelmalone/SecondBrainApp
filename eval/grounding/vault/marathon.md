# Marathon Training

The weekly long run is the backbone of the plan. Add no more than a mile or two
each week so the legs adapt without breaking down, and keep the pace easy enough
to hold a conversation.

Three weeks out, begin the taper: cut total mileage sharply while keeping a
little intensity so the legs stay sharp and fully recovered on race day.

Practice your fueling on the long runs — gels and water every forty-five minutes
— so race morning holds no surprises for your stomach.
